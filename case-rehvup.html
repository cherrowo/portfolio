Designing a gamification system from zero

📅 When: Sep – Dec 2023 👤 Role: UX researcher / product designer / information architect 🏢 Where: RehvUp (workplace social app)

What is RehvUp? It's a workplace social media platform, halfway between Reddit and Instagram but for employee wellbeing; with managers, communities, and EX coaches (think influencers that make content around wellness in the workplace). I was brought in as the sole designer to fix a broken UX and then build a fully new gamification system from scratch. Since the startup was acquired 3 months after my work was implemented, I assume I did a great job.

Context

The platform and the problem

RehvUp lets employees interact with short-form video content, join communities, and engage with EX coaches on workplace topics like mindfulness and productivity. Something like Instagram but internal to a company. The app has four key user types: regular employees, managers, communities, and EX coaches who create content.

When I joined, the app had a backlog of user complaints. Navigation was confusing, post engagement logic was broken, and user journeys weren't clear. On top of fixing all of that, there was a second major goal: design an engagement system that would give users a reason to come back regularly.

Phase 1

Information Architecture (IA) audit and complaint reduction

Before anything new could be designed, the existing problems needed to be understood. I worked directly with third-party engineering contractors using insights gained through app testing sessions, auditing logic flows and tracing the root causes of recurring complaints.

🔍

App testing sessions

Walked through the app systematically, documenting every confusing flow, broken interaction, and unclear label

🗺️

IA restructure

Remapped the information architecture so users could navigate without guidance, tested and iterated through multiple feedback rounds

⚙️

Logic fixes

Identified and resolved post engagement logic errors (interactions that produced unexpected outcomes or no feedback at all)

✅

Result: 90% fewer complaints

Fixing navigation structure and post logic resolved the vast majority of reported issues before the gamification work even began

Phase 2

Gamification system design

With the core app in a better state, I moved to the new project: design a gamification system that would motivate regular participation and give users a sense of progress. I started by defining the system architecture: what the components were, how they connected, and what user behaviour each was meant to drive

⭐

Experience Points (XP)

Earned through app interactions: posting, commenting, watching content. Displayed on the user profile to visualise progress toward the next persona tier.

🏅

Personas (Ranks)

Rank tiers unlocked by accumulated XP. Each persona has defined privileges and benefits. The goal is to make progression feel like it means something, not just a number going up.

🏆

Leaderboards

Weekly and monthly boards showing the most active users. Additional XP awarded to top performers, creating a healthy competitive loop without being punishing.

📊

Progress Dashboard

A profile-level view of points, badges earned, current rank, and progress to the next tier. Gives users a clear picture of where they are and what comes next.

🎯

Challenges

Short-term and long-term goals for bonus XP. Short challenges renew frequently to keep daily engagement up; longer ones reward sustained commitment.

🎖️

Milestone Badges

Goal-oriented badges tied to specific app milestones (first post, 30-day streak, etc). Gives users clear targets to aim for beyond the XP accumulation loop.

📚

Educational Resources

FAQs, tutorials, and tips explaining how the gamification system works. Helps users understand how to maximise engagement, especially important for a new system with no prior reference.

Scope document

What's in! (and what isn't)

The scope document defined exactly what the project covered and what it did not. Scoping out the admin panel, notifications, and EX coach panel upfront prevented scope creep and kept engineering focused during handoff.

Deliverables

What I created

🎨 Medium-fidelity Figma prototype

Full mobile gamification flow (XP display, persona progression, leaderboard views, challenge cards, badge gallery, and the profile dashboard). Focused on functionality over pixel-perfect branding.

📄 Scope documentation

Formal scope document covering project objectives, feature list, integration boundaries, quality standards, and a defined approval process. Executives provided feedback before development began.

🖥️ Desktop companion interface

Translated the mobile-first experience to desktop (same system, different layout constraints). Ensured consistency between platforms.

🗺️ User personas + roadmap

Designed user personas for each of the four user types, and a product roadmap prioritising fixes and new features across the term.
